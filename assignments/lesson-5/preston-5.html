Is This What's Making You Sneeze?

by Pollen.com

If breathing in fresh spring air makes you sneeze, or sitting on a grassy hill gives you a rash, you may have a grass pollen allergy. Grass pollens are microscopic and travel easily through the air. Everyone breathes in grass pollen, but not everyone reacts to it. If you have a grass allergy and go outside on a day when pollen is in the air, you're likely to be irritated by symptoms like watery eyes or a runny nose. Some people can also have a reaction just by touching grass. If you're severely allergic, you could even experience anaphylaxis. Continue to Full Article
